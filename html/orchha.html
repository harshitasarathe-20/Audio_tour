Majestic Orchha

Orchha literally means ‘hidden place’ and it stays true to its name! Located in Madhya Pradesh, Orchha isn’t on most tourist maps and several travelers club their visit to Orchha with a trip to Jhansi (16 km away) or Khajuraho (170 km away). But with beautiful monuments, both big and small, revealing tales of battles between kings and emperors, Orchha deserves a trip of its own.

Once the capital of the Bundela Rajput kings, Orchha was established by Maharaja Rudra Pratap Singh in 1501. The medieval city has since seemingly frozen in time. Its several monuments continue to retain their original grandeur even as the city itself seems quite content to be hidden away from the throngs of tourists that descend upon Madhya Pradesh year after year.

The historic town of Orchha, nestled on the banks of river Betwa.Here, the river Betwa splits into seven channels, also called the Satdhara. Legend goes that this is in honour of the seven erstwhile Chiefs of Orchha.

Orchha Fort
It is also known as “Jahangir’s Palace” located inside the Fort of Agra built for his son Jahangir to use as a residence for his stay. It symbolizes the Mughal Architecture of 16th century made up of red stone and white marble. It is one of the buildings were entering you will see the Agra Fort when visited gate via Amar Singh.

Ram Raja Temple
IThis is one of the important tourist points for the travelers. It is one of the fascinating temples in Orchha, which has historical tales behind it. This was a palace for the ruler Madhukar Shah at years ago. This temple is the only temple where Lord Rama is worshipped as King Rama as it holds a mark of religious importance

Raja Mahal
Raja Mahal is also known as “Raja Mandir.” The Mahal is built by Rudra Pratap Singh which started during the 16th century and ended on 17th century. The Mahal is full of lavish accommodations with elevated balconies. The interiors of the Mahal is of mirrors and murals which are plastered on the ceilings and walls of the Mahal. These interiors make a great impact on the art enthusiast.

Chhatris
It is also known as Chattris or Cenotaphs. Chattris is situated at the bank of Betwa River. There are about 14 Chattris, which are grouped in a row along the Kanchan Ghat of the Betwa River, which marks as a respect for the blue blood of mighty rulers of Bundelkhand. These chattris are tombs of maharajas that have been elevated on the platforms and supported by pillars. Its unique architecture style and beautiful design makes an ideal place for tourist attractions.

Best Places To Visit

Ram Raja Temple in Orchha is the only temple where Lord Rama is worshipped as a king.

The 'Chhatris' or cenotaphs of Orchha were constructed in honour of its rulers. These are grouped along the beautiful River Betwa.

Orchha Retreat

Dedicated to lord Vishnu, Chaturbhuj Temple is the main tourist attraction in Orchha.

Lakshmi Narain Temple - An intriguing mix of a fort and a temple, its walls are elaborately decorated with fabulous mural paintings, which show compositions from mythological themes.

Sound & Light show is conducted every evening to enthuse those interested in knowing the rich history of Orchha.

The Orchha bird sanctuary is a paradise for bird-watchers as it is home to various types of in-house and migrant birds.

River Rafting in Betwa River offers one of its kind experience. You can do sightseeing of the popular Chhatris of Orchha as you raft.
