Holkar Fort

Listen Audio

Ahilya Fort, in the central Indian town of Maheshwar, sits high above the sacred river Narmada. Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar ruled here from 1765 to 1796 and built Ahilya Wada, her personal residences, offices, and darbaar audience hall, within the fort.
The majestic 250 years old Ahilya Fort sits on the edge of a cliff, high above the sacred Narmada River, surrounded by the beautiful and unexploited town of Maheshwar. The views over the river and ghats are exceptional.
This palace is the zenith of Maratha architecture. One can marvel at the Chhatris as well as the palace that houses a beautiful life-size statue of Rani Ahilya Bai Holkar sitting on a throne. The fort premises also houses several temples dedicated to various incarnations of Lord Shiva. Situated within the Maheshwar Fort, Rajwada is the palace where Devi Ahilyabai used to reside. It is a small museum now and houses things related to queen Ahilyabai. With its splendid architecture of the Maratha period, this fort attracts a large number of tourists from all over the globe.
In the year 2000, Prince Richard Holkar, her descendant and son of the last Maharaja of Indore, converted his home in Ahilya Wada into a guest residence, today known around the world as the Ahilya Fort Hotel.
